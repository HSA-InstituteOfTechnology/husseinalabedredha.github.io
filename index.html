About Me

I am an Economics & Mathematics student specializing in applied econometrics, financial modeling, and quantitative analysis. My work bridges academic research with real-world applications in credit risk, portfolio analytics, and corporate finance.

I focus on econometric modeling, causal inference, land-use and housing supply constraints, and market-driven scenario analysis. My academic interests include instrumental variable strategies, policy-driven investment responses, and the development of synthetic constraint indices for urban markets.

Beyond research, I have hands-on experience in portfolio reconciliation, dealership credit analysis, valuation modeling, and building a private investment fund. My goal is to continue producing rigorous research while advancing analytically-driven work in finance.
